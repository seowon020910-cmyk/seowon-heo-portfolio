Background & Objective

Costume as visual storytelling

This project was developed through the University Innovation Support Program for Young Creators as part of the costume design team.

It aimed to explore visual storytelling in theatre — integrating costume, movement, and stage design to deliver an emotionally cohesive narrative.

Through this process, I learned to approach costume design as a visual language that shapes both performance and audience experience.

"A university-funded original musical project that explores how costume design can translate character emotion and narrative tone through colour, texture, and movement on stage."

The poster visual — soft gradients and moonlight tones — was designed to express nostalgia and the feeling of floating through memory. This concept guided the overall stage atmosphere and costume palette.

Process

From analysis to stage

  • 01

    Character & Script Analysis

    Before designing, I analysed the script, character relationships, and time period to understand each role's emotional tone and visual identity. This research helped define each character's personality through costume, fabric choice, and colour palette.

    Character and script analysis documentation

  • 02

    Costume Styling & Fitting

    Based on the analysis, I developed multiple costume combinations in the wardrobe studio to reflect character traits and scene transitions. Initial fittings were conducted with performers to confirm fit, movement, and stage compatibility — followed by secondary fittings during rehearsals.

    First and second costume fittings with performers

  • 03

    Rehearsal Coordination

    During weekly rehearsals, I coordinated with the director and cast to ensure that each costume aligned with character expression, movement, and comfort. I participated in multiple fitting sessions to verify that costumes maintained balance under stage lighting and movement constraints. Feedback from actors was promptly reflected to enhance performance quality and realism.

    Weekly rehearsal plan showing costume checks, fitting schedules, and scene run-through coordination

  • 04

    Technical Rehearsal & Setup Week

    During the technical rehearsal and setup week, I checked costume conditions under stage lighting and made final adjustments for performance readiness. Quick-change points were rehearsed with the stage management and lighting crew to ensure seamless scene transitions. This phase focused on maintaining timing precision, costume accuracy, and backstage coordination during live shows.

    SET-UP schedule illustrating costume change workflow, lighting alignment, and final-stage operation

  • 05

    Performance & Live Management

    During the technical rehearsal period, I refined costume details based on lighting cues, stage movement, and performer blocking — ensuring each costume behaved consistently under live-performance conditions. Before the dress rehearsal, I prepared identical costume presets for each performer and coordinated quick-change timings with the stage management team, ensuring smooth transitions across consecutive performances.

Achievements & Reflections

What I learned

Through this project, I strengthened my ability to respond quickly to costume adjustments during SET-UP week and live rehearsals. Working with performers and stage staff helped me understand how costume fit and movement change under real performance conditions.

In the musical format, I paid close attention to performer mobility — ensuring that costumes supported choreography and stage blocking without discomfort. This experience deepened my understanding of how functional design can enhance character expression.

Overall, the project enhanced both the practical and artistic aspects of my costume design approach, providing a strong foundation for future production work.
