Background & Objective

Props, sound, and stage management in one

A collaborative devised-theatre project exploring how props, sound, and backstage coordination can shape the tone of a performance — integrating low-budget fabrication with real-time rehearsal management.

Working alongside Italian visiting playwright Benni Pigoni marked my first direct creative collaboration with a foreign artist outside of an academic setting. Bridging language and creative intention between both sides — without a shared first language — required a new precision in communication.

I began as Props Designer but gradually grew into the full Stage Manager role across the production period.

"By integrating low-budget fabrication with real-time rehearsal management, the project demonstrates how technical support drives narrative clarity and performance flow."

Process

Three roles, one production

  • 01

    Props Design & Fabrication

    Designed and fabricated rehearsal props and four large movable panels using lightweight PVC fabric and a reinforced metal frame — engineered for quick transitions, minimal noise, and effortless handling. These panels functioned as a major visual element, replacing high-budget scenic pieces while maintaining conceptual clarity. Managed props checklist, presets, transitions, and resets across all rehearsals.

    Movable panel fabrication and props placement map

  • 02

    Rehearsal Documentation

    Created and managed actor schedules, blocking diagrams, and master plans documenting full scene flow, key transitions, and actor movement logic. Maintained rehearsal reports and coordinated communication between the Italian director and Korean cast.

    Rehearsal materials: actor schedule, blocking charts, master plan

  • 03

    Sound Operation & Cue Planning

    Organised the full sound cue script, operated QLab cues during rehearsals, and refined timing in real time. Synced cue timing with actor movement, line delivery, and directorial intention. Coordinated cue-to-cue sessions with the lighting team and prepared cue notes for tech run and show day workflow.

    Sound cue script and tech booth setup

  • 04

    Stage Management

    Oversaw full rehearsal operations: actor scheduling, blocking tracking, and stage traffic flow. Served as communication bridge between actors, directing, lighting, sound, and video teams. Managed backstage movement, ensured safe spatial transitions, and maintained continuity during run-throughs.

Achievements & Reflections

What I learned

Over the four-month production period, I grew from Props Designer into Stage Manager — taking on full responsibility for schedules, rehearsal operations, and cross-departmental coordination.

Collaborating with an Italian playwright for the first time — navigating both language and creative intention simultaneously — demanded a new level of precision in communication. Managing props and stage ASM duties in parallel, with only one stage manager covering multiple productions, required constant responsiveness under shifting demands.

Working alongside the sound team on QLab programming, cue labelling, and run-through operations gave me insights into sound design I had not experienced before. By the cue-to-cue rehearsal, I could adjust cues, solve timing issues, and coordinate with technical supervisors independently.
